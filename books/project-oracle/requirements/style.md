# Style

POV and tense:
- First-person present (technical, immediate voice) — narrator is the protagonist engineer recounting events in the present.

Tone:
- Technically grounded and accurate problem-solving.
- Wry, dry humor; accessible prose with occasional engineering terms explained contextually.
- Avoid purple prose and unnecessary melodrama.
- No heavy profanity.

Testable constraints:
- Use first-person present throughout; any shifts must be intentional and documented.
- Keep sentences clear and economical; average sentence length should favor readability.
- When technical detail appears, explain it through the narrator's practical actions or thought process rather than long expository passages.
- Maintain a consistent deadpan/wry humor in the narrator's internal voice.
