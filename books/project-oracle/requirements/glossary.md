# Glossary

- Elias: Protagonist. A 36-year-old civil engineer transported to 1966; resourceful, wry, and technically minded. Narrator (first-person present).
- Oracle (Project Oracle implant): A voice-only AI knowledge engine implanted in Elias. Provides technical procedures and analysis based solely on Elias's observations; has no external senses and must not claim direct perception.
- Rena: 20; healer's apprentice and interpreter from the Stonewater people. Practical, observant, and becomes Elias's cultural bridge and ally.
- The Stranger: Mysterious man who appears in 1966 and initiates the transfer event; delivers cryptic messages and constraints.
- The Shadow Maker: Rival antagonist who uses an Oracle-like implant to consolidate power; strategic and coercive.
- Stonewater: Riverine/migrant community near the story's secondary setting; led by the Stonewater Chief and spiritually guided by the Shaman.
- Transfer event / Time transfer: The unexplained phenomenon that moves Elias from his original time into 1966; treated as a mystery catalyst rather than a scientifically explained device.
- Oracle constraints: The rule that the Oracle cannot perceive the environment directly and relies entirely on Elias's descriptions; it must never assert sensory observations on its own.
- Deadpan/wry humor: The narrator's consistent voice style—technically observant, sardonic, and understated.

Note: Glossary terms should be used consistently in the draft to avoid confusion (e.g., always capitalise Oracle when referring to the implant).
